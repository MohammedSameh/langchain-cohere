8905A Trident System Advanced Maintenance
['Professional']
This intensive course provides advanced knowledge on Trident™ system maintenance. Course topics help you identify and respond to internal, external, and field faults. The course reviews basic Trident system implementation, maintenance, and troubleshooting.&nbsp;<br><br>To gain practical experience, you use Triconex™ Enhanced Diagnostic Monitor, in Microsoft® Windows® based TriStation™ 1131 Developer’s Workbench software, to troubleshoot, respond to alarms, replace modules, clear faults, and work with reporting.<br><br>Lab exercises and written evaluations help validate your understanding of maintenance scenarios and troubleshooting using advanced techniques.<br><br>The course requires the availability of specific hardware at the participant’s location. The instructor demonstrates system functionality virtually, using remote hardware and live video. To perform course activities, each participant requires access to physical hardware. Before registering for this course, your local Process Automation Learning Services representative can discuss hardware requirements and available support options with you.<br><br><strong>Learning Objectives</strong><br><br>After completing this course, you can:<br>• Describe the basic theory of operation of Triple Modular Redundancy (TMR) architecture in the Trident system.<br>• Describe the operational concepts and basic features of TriStation 1131 software.<br>• Recognize and respond to internal, external, and system faults.<br>• Disable and force points during system maintenance.<br>• Connect to and navigate Enhanced Diagnostic Monitor.<br>• Display firmware status.<br>• Collect system events.<br>• Use advanced maintenance techniques to troubleshoot a Trident system.<br><br><strong>Prerequisites<br></strong><br>• Working knowledge of Programmable Logic Controllers (PLCs) in a safety environment<br>• Familiarity with basic electronics and maintenance procedures<br>• 8905 Trident Basic Maintenance (recommended)
