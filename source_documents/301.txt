9006 Introduction to Ethernet and TCP-IP (Virtual)
['Essential']
This course provides basic knowledge to users who have little or no previous networking experience. Course content is set at a level most suited to inexperienced programmers. If you are more experienced in the fundamentals of networking, you may find you are able to complete this course before the designated duration.<br><br>This course comprises Day 1 of the more extensive course 9003 Industrial Networking.<br><br><strong>Learning Objectives<br></strong><br>After completing this course, you can:&nbsp;<br>• Provide an accurate historical account of computer networking and outline the basic components of networking systems.<br>• Describe the uses of each of the 7 layers of the Open System Interconnection (OSI) model.<br>• Describe 2 primary network cabling technologies—unshielded twisted pair and optical fiber—and the earlier technology coaxial.<br>• Implement Ethernet networking, with an understanding of the various limits applicable to this technology.<br>• Describe features and compare differences of the 3 major addressing schemes: MAC, IP, and DNS names.<br>• Define the roles and uses of various network connectivity devices, such as hubs, routers, and gateways.<br>• Implement a TCP/IP addressing scheme with correct use of subnet masks.<br><br><strong>Prerequisites<br></strong><br>Familiarity with Microsoft® Windows® operating systems
