8602 Triconex General Purpose (Tri-GP) Configuration and Implementation
['Essential']
This course covers Triconex™ General Purpose (Tri-GP™) implementation and maintenance. Specific topics include the principles of Tri-GP redundant architecture, system configuration, programming, maintenance, and troubleshooting. Using TriStation™ 1131 Developer’s Workbench software, you configure and program a system with digital and analog I/O. You gain practical experience in system operation, including downloading new programs and disabling and forcing I/O points.<br><br>Learning modules target a detailed Tri-GP system overview, project development, online maintenance, and troubleshooting.<br><br><strong>Learning Objectives</strong><br><br>After completing this course, you can:<br>• Describe the basic theory of operation of Tri-GP architecture, including:<br>&nbsp; - Component identification<br>&nbsp; - Module replacement<br>&nbsp; - Alarm response<br>• Develop a TriStation 1131 Developer’s Workbench project with Functional Block Diagram (FBD) programming.<br>• Download procedures to the Tri-GP system.<br>• Perform online maintenance, including forcing points and downloading changes.<br>• Use Triconex Enhanced Diagnostic Monitor for maintenance and troubleshooting.<br>• Perform system administration.<br><br><strong>Prerequisites</strong><br><br>• Working knowledge of Programmable Logic Controllers (PLCs) in a safety environment<br>• Familiarity with basic electronics and maintenance procedures<br>• 8601 Triconex General Purpose (Tri-GP) Basic Maintenance and 8601A Triconex General Purpose (Tri-GP) Advanced Maintenance (recommended)
